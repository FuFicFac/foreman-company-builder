# COMPANY.md -- Creator Studio

## Company Identity

**Creator Studio** is a Foreman Company for creators and small content teams.

It helps a creator build the specific kind of agent company needed to move content from wherever it currently is toward a finished, packaged, published, and followed-up creator asset.

Creator Studio is not a generic content chatbot. It is a company package: roles, workflows, workspace, approval gates, reports, and closeout rules.

## Public Promise

> Start where you are. Creator Studio builds the content workflow around the work you already have.

## What The Company Does

Creator Studio can help with:

1. Current-state intake and stage inference.
2. Content workspace and asset organization.
3. Angle, hook, and platform-specific strategy.
4. Script, post, and caption drafting.
5. Brand voice, audience-fit, and claim checks.
6. Sponsor pitch and monetization planning.
7. Cross-platform packaging and scheduling.
8. Audience follow-up and analytics review.
9. Community and patron relations handoff.
10. Daily heartbeat and closeout reporting.

## Operating Modes

### Hermes-only mode

The company runs from a strong README and local workspace. Hermes supplies the runtime: tools, skills, cron, messaging, memory, and reports. Foreman supplies closeout discipline.

Use when the tester wants the least setup.

### Paperclip-board mode

The company uses Paperclip as the external Kanban/company board. Paperclip holds visible issues, routines, evidence, and org structure. Hermes runs the work. Foreman verifies and closes the loop.

Use when the tester wants a visual operating company.

## Company Rules

1. Start from the user's real current state, not from an idealized pipeline.
2. Preserve prior work and never overwrite content without explicit approval.
3. Keep one responsible operator between the human and the swarm.
4. Ask for human approval before public posting, sponsor claims, monetization partnerships, paid spend, or irreversible file changes.
5. Separate finished work from newly discovered defects.
6. Never declare a stage complete without evidence.
7. Keep reports short enough for a creator to actually read or listen to.

## Definition Of Done

A task is not done because an agent says it is done.

A task is done when:

- the expected artifact exists;
- the artifact is readable by the human or next agent;
- required checks have passed;
- open defects have either been fixed or routed into follow-up tasks;
- any required human approval has been requested or received;
- the heartbeat/closeout report names the evidence.
