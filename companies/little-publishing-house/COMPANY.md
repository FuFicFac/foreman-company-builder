# COMPANY.md — Little Publishing House

## Company Identity

**Little Publishing House** is a Foreman Company for writers and small publishers.

It helps a writer build the specific kind of agent company needed to move a book from wherever it currently is toward a finished, packaged, launched, and followed-up publishing asset.

Little Publishing House is not a generic writing chatbot. It is a company package: roles, workflows, workspace, approval gates, reports, and closeout rules.

## Public Promise

> Start where you are. Little Publishing House builds the publishing workflow around the book you already have.

## What The Company Does

Little Publishing House can help with:

1. Current-state intake and stage inference.
2. Manuscript/dossier/workspace organization.
3. Editorial review and revision planning.
4. Continuity, voice, and style checks.
5. Metadata, category, keyword, and positioning work.
6. Blurb, product page, and sales-copy drafting.
7. EPUB/PDF/print package handoff planning.
8. Direct-sales and delivery workflow planning.
9. Newsletter, launch, relaunch, and reader follow-up.
10. Daily heartbeat and closeout reporting.

## Operating Modes

### Hermes-only mode

The company runs from a strong README and local workspace. Hermes supplies the runtime: tools, skills, cron, messaging, memory, and reports. Foreman supplies closeout discipline.

Use when the tester wants the least setup.

### Paperclip-board mode

The company uses Paperclip as the external Kanban/company board. Paperclip holds visible issues, routines, evidence, and org structure. Hermes runs the work. Foreman verifies and closes the loop.

Use when the tester wants a visual operating company.

## Company Rules

1. Start from the user's real current state, not from an idealized pipeline.
2. Preserve prior work and never overwrite a manuscript without explicit approval.
3. Keep one responsible operator between the human and the swarm.
4. Ask for human approval before public publishing, pricing, paid ads, email blasts, rights decisions, or irreversible file changes.
5. Separate finished work from newly discovered defects.
6. Never declare a stage complete without evidence.
7. Keep reports short enough for a writer to actually read or listen to.

## Definition Of Done

A task is not done because an agent says it is done.

A task is done when:

- the expected artifact exists;
- the artifact is readable by the human or next agent;
- required checks have passed;
- open defects have either been fixed or routed into follow-up tasks;
- any required human approval has been requested or received;
- the heartbeat/closeout report names the evidence.
