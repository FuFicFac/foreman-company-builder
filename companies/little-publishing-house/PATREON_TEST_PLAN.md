# Little Publishing House — Patreon Tester Plan

## Goal

Give Patreon testers early access to a guided agent-company kit for one book project, without pretending the full Foreman/Paperclip product is finished.

The test is not: “Can this replace a publisher?”

The test is:

> Can Little Publishing House make a writer's current book project clearer, more organized, and easier to move forward in one focused session?

## Ideal First Testers

- Writers with an idea, outline, partial manuscript, finished draft, or launch mess.
- Comfortable sharing a small excerpt or project summary.
- Willing to give feedback on clarity, usefulness, and trust.
- Not expecting legal, financial, or guaranteed publishing advice.

## What Testers Get

A lightweight package:

1. A strong README explaining their book/company setup.
2. A workspace template.
3. A stage diagnosis.
4. One focused deliverable.
5. A closeout report with evidence and next steps.

Optional mode choices:

- **Hermes-only mode** — simplest; everything can run from README/workspace/report.
- **Paperclip-board mode** — for testers who want a visible Kanban/company board if/when available.

## First Tester Flow

```text
1. Intake form / message
2. Create project workspace
3. Stage inference
4. Confirm stage with tester
5. Produce one deliverable
6. Closeout report
7. Feedback questions
```

## First Deliverable Options

Pick one per tester:

- Book project map.
- Revision plan.
- Metadata/product-page package.
- Launch checklist.
- Backlist/relaunch diagnosis.
- Manuscript excerpt editorial pass.
- Publishing workspace cleanup.

## Feedback Questions

Ask after delivery:

1. Did this help you understand where your book is?
2. Did the next step feel concrete?
3. Did the report feel too technical, too vague, or useful?
4. Would you want this as a recurring publishing company?
5. Would you prefer a visible board, or are reports enough?
6. What would make you trust it more?
7. Would you pay for this as software, service, course, or membership perk?

## Boundaries

Do not promise:

- guaranteed publishing success;
- legal rights advice;
- tax/accounting advice;
- finished cover art;
- retailer upload without human review;
- fully autonomous publishing.

Do promise:

- organization;
- momentum;
- clear next steps;
- evidence-based reports;
- human approval before public or irreversible actions.

## Success Criteria

A good first test succeeds if the writer says:

> “I know what stage my book is in, I know what to do next, and I trust the system enough to come back.”
