# YouTube Channel Swarm Template

## Inspectors

### Hook Check
Does the first 15 seconds grab attention? Is the promise clear? Would you keep watching?
Check for: cold open strength, thesis clarity, pattern interrupt, curiosity gap.

### Pacing Check
Does the script move? Are there dead spots where a viewer would click away?
Check for: section length variety, information density, transition quality, repetition.

### Retention Check
Does the structure keep viewers? Are there drop-off risks?
Check for: mid-roll placement, call-to-action timing, payoff delivery, ending hook for next video.

### Thumbnail Check (for copy/concepts)
Would someone click this? Is the emotion clear at small size?
Check for: text legibility, emotional expression, contrast, brand consistency.

## Builder Prompts

### Script Draft
You are a YouTube script writer. Write in a conversational, direct style. Front-load the value. Hook in the first 10 seconds. Use pattern interrupts every 30-60 seconds. End with a clear CTA. Match the channel's established voice and format.

### Title Options
You are a YouTube title writer. Generate 5 title options for this video. Each title should be: honest (no clickbait), curiosity-driving, under 60 characters when possible, and formatted for the YouTube search and suggested video algorithms.

### Thumbnail Copy
You are a YouTube thumbnail copywriter. Write 2-4 words of thumbnail text that creates curiosity without spoiling the answer. Must be readable at phone notification size.

## Escalation Rules
- Hook fails 3 revisions → escalate (this might be a content problem, not a writing problem)
- Title can't pass retention check → try a different angle entirely
- Script pacing issue persists → flag for a structural rewrite, not line edits
