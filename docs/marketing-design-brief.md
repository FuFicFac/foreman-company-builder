# Foreman + Little Publishing House Marketing / Design Brief

Use this brief to create focused website mockups, brand explorations, and icon/logo directions for Foreman and its flagship Foreman Company: Little Publishing House.

## Core strategic decision

Foreman should not launch as an abstract everything-platform first.

Foreman is the platform and logic layer, but the first emotionally clear product should be **Little Publishing House**, because authors and publishing are the domain ej knows deeply and can teach credibly.

The launch strategy is:

```text
Foreman = platform / company-builder logic
A Foreman Company = branded category / package type
Little Publishing House = flagship Foreman Company for authors
Lessons / Second Brain training = optional education layer, not a product requirement
```

## Foreman positioning

Foreman is the system that turns AI agents into accountable companies.

Foreman can eventually pull from the user’s project files, connected tools, web sources, accounts, and context to assemble the company they need for the work they are doing.

Foreman’s long-term promise:

> Build AI companies that know what they’re doing.

Alternate Foreman lines:

- Foreman keeps agent companies honest.
- Turn AI agents into accountable companies.
- Build the AI company your project needs.
- From agent chaos to company discipline.
- A company-builder for AI work.
- Foreman gives agents workflows, inspections, tools, escalation, and daily check-ins.

Foreman is not only a Kanban board, not only an agent runner, and not only a dashboard. It is the operating-discipline layer that gives AI work structure.

## Little Publishing House positioning

Little Publishing House is the first flagship Foreman Company.

It is for authors and writers.

It is not “generic AI writing software.”
It is not “write my book for me.”
It is not “you need to become a project manager.”
It is not “you need a giant Second Brain.”

Little Publishing House is:

> A little publishing house around your book.

Core promise:

> Start where you are.

Expanded promise:

> Little Publishing House is a Foreman Company for writers. Whether you have an idea, a rough draft, a finished manuscript, a launch already in motion, or a published book that needs follow-up, it finds your stage and builds the next publishing workflow around it.

Author-facing copy:

> You do not need to know where to start. Little Publishing House helps figure out where your book already is, what work is already done, what still matters, and what should happen next.

## The key product principle

Do not force a linear start.

Little Publishing House must not imply that every author begins by dropping in a draft.

Users can enter at any stage:

- idea
- premise
- notes
- outline
- partial draft
- rough draft
- finished manuscript
- developmental edit
- revision
- copyedit
- proofread
- metadata / positioning
- cover / creative direction
- storefront / distribution
- launch
- reader follow-up
- analytics
- relaunch

Universal Foreman Company rule:

```text
Discover current state → infer stage → confirm with human → start from there → preserve prior work
```

This rule applies to every future Foreman Company, not just publishing.

## Second Brain clarification

Second Brain is not required.

The confusion came from a possible education/lesson offer where ej teaches authors his deeper personal operating system, including Second Brain-style knowledge workflows.

Separate these layers:

```text
Foreman = platform
Little Publishing House = flagship author product
Second Brain / Author Operating System = optional lesson, advanced workflow, or upsell
```

Product copy should say:

> Each book gets a simple workspace and living wiki.

Do not say:

> You need a Second Brain.

Good explanation:

> Each book gets a small living wiki so the company remembers what matters: summary, characters, themes, style notes, publishing decisions, open questions, launch choices, and reader context.

## Audience

Primary audience:

- authors
- indie writers
- genre writers
- newsletter writers
- nonfiction authors
- fiction authors
- introverted creators
- people overwhelmed by the business/process around a book
- writers who understand AI might help but do not want generic AI slop
- people who need structure, accountability, and gentle momentum

Secondary audience:

- AI builders who want to understand what a Foreman Company is
- creator-operators who might later want their own company template
- course/community buyers who want to learn ej’s process

Audience truth:

Authors do not wake up wanting “multi-agent orchestration.”
They wake up wanting the book to move without feeling alone, confused, or buried under publishing chores.

## YouTube / marketing angle

This should be designed as something ej can explain and demo in public.

The product must be visually understandable on YouTube in 5–10 seconds.

The demo story should be:

1. “Here is a book project.”
2. “Here is where I actually am in the process.”
3. “Little Publishing House figures that out.”
4. “It creates the workflow around my book.”
5. “It shows what needs me today.”
6. “Foreman is the logic underneath that keeps the company honest.”

Good video title energy:

- I Gave My Book Its Own Publishing House
- I Built an AI Publishing Company Around My Manuscript
- This AI Doesn’t Write My Book — It Runs the Publishing Workflow
- My Book Has a Staff Now
- I Built a Little Publishing House with AI Agents
- The Missing Layer for AI Authors Isn’t Writing — It’s Workflow

Marketing thesis:

> The next step for AI authors is not another text generator. It is a visible publishing workflow around the book.

## Site architecture

Design two focused pages, not one muddled page.

### Page 1: Foreman homepage

Purpose:
Explain the parent platform and category.

Foreman homepage should answer:

- What is Foreman?
- What is a Foreman Company?
- Why do AI agents need operating discipline?
- How does Foreman build or run specialized companies?
- Why is Little Publishing House the flagship example?

Foreman homepage hero option:

Headline:
> Build AI companies that know what they’re doing.

Subhead:
> Foreman turns agents, tools, files, connected services, and web context into accountable companies with workflows, inspections, escalation rules, daily check-ins, and visible status.

Primary CTA:
> Explore Little Publishing House

Secondary CTA:
> See how Foreman works

Foreman homepage sections:

1. Hero — Foreman as the company-builder.
2. Problem — AI agents are powerful, but without discipline they become chaos.
3. What is a Foreman Company? — a specialized AI company with roles, tools, workflow, status, and checks.
4. Core loop — discover state → assemble company → assign work → inspect → escalate → report.
5. Featured Foreman Company — Little Publishing House.
6. Under the hood — Paperclip shows the company, Hermes runs it, Foreman provides discipline, Printing Press supplies tools.
7. Future company examples — Newsletter Studio, Research Office, Course House, Software Workshop. Keep these loose; do not overbuild them yet.
8. CTA — Start with Little Publishing House / Build a Foreman Company.

Foreman visual language:

- calm, capable, structured
- workshop / office / command table energy
- not cyberpunk
- not pure enterprise SaaS
- more “beautiful operating room for AI companies” than “hacker dashboard”
- diagrams are welcome, but they should feel editorial and legible

Foreman visual metaphors:

- blueprint + ledger
- workshop bench
- company org chart
- routing board
- inspection stamps
- status lights
- annotated workflow cards
- compass / map / dispatcher board

Foreman logo/icon ideas to explore:

1. A hardhat subtly shaped like an “F” — probably too literal but worth sketching.
2. A clipboard/checkmark combined with a small house/company roof.
3. A routing-node diagram inside a square stamp.
4. A foreman’s mark: “F” monogram with inspection tick.
5. A simplified crane hook lifting connected workflow cards.
6. A compass/level tool forming an F.
7. A stamp/seal that says “Checked by Foreman” without text in the icon.
8. A small factory/office silhouette made of cards/nodes.
9. A ledger page with one highlighted decision path.
10. A modular block icon: agent, tool, task, check.

## Things to avoid

Avoid:

- generic AI SaaS gradient soup
- black neon hacker dashboards
- excessive product-name pileups above the fold
- corporate “10x productivity” language
- making Little Publishing House look like a traditional publisher that owns rights or distributes books directly
- implying the product writes the book for the author
- implying the author must start with a completed draft
- requiring Second Brain
- making Foreman too abstract on the homepage
- making Little Publishing House explain all of Foreman’s infrastructure too early

## Final strategic guardrail

Foreman should be broad enough to become the company-builder platform.

Little Publishing House should be specific enough to sell, demo, teach, and emotionally land with authors.

The homepage should make people understand Foreman.

The Little Publishing House subpage should make people want one.
