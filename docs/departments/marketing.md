# Marketing (Universal Department)

## Purpose

Research the market, position the offer, and produce copy and campaigns across SEO, email, social, ads, launch, and conversion-rate optimization. This department turns strategy and product into demand: it owns how the company is perceived, how offers are framed, and how each channel performs against measurable conversion.

It is the **demand + positioning layer** that ensures the rest of the company's work reaches the right audience with the right promise. Where Executive sets the mission and Product builds the offer, Marketing translates both into channel-appropriate creative and runs the campaigns that test, refine, and scale acquisition.

This department operates lean by default — fast brief-to-launch cycles with inspection gates on brand, clarity, conversion, compliance, and channel fit before anything ships publicly.

## Universal responsibilities

- **Define positioning and messaging**: own the value proposition, audience framing, and competitive positioning that every channel inherits.
- **Produce channel-appropriate copy and creative direction**: draft ad copy, email sequences, landing pages, and social posts that match each channel's conventions and constraints.
- **Plan and execute launches and campaigns**: run the campaign cycle from brief through performance review, with explicit launch dates and budget gates.
- **Measure conversion and channel fit**: track per-channel performance, attribute results, and kill or scale based on evidence.
- **Maintain brand consistency**: enforce brand voice, claims, and visual direction across every external touchpoint.

## Core workflows

### 1) Campaign build and launch

**Inputs**
- a new-campaign trigger (product launch, promotion, always-on acquisition)
- brand brief / positioning doc
- budget and channel constraints
- target audience and conversion goal

**Process (stages)**
- `brief`: capture objective, audience, offer, budget, success metric, and stop conditions.
- `positioning`: frame the offer against the market and current positioning doc.
- `copy-draft`: produce channel-appropriate copy (`ad-copy`, `email-sequence`, `landing-page`, `social-posts`).
- `creative-direction`: align visual direction and brand voice; run brand-check and clarity-check.
- `launch`: publish to channels after compliance-check, channel-fit, and human approval on final-copy and launch-date.
- `performance-review`: measure conversion and channel fit; decide kill, hold, or scale; write performance memo.

**Outputs (evidence artifacts)**
- campaign-brief (objective, audience, offer, budget, metric, stop conditions)
- launch-artifacts (published copy, creative, channel links)
- performance-memo (results vs goal, channel verdict, next action)

## Required capabilities (department-level)

- **marketing**: Positioning, copy, campaigns, SEO, social, ads, CRO — the core capability that authorizes this department to brief, draft, launch, and measure campaigns.

## Optional capabilities

- **analytics-reporting**: per-channel attribution, funnel analytics, and performance dashboards (Google Ads, Dub, GA4, PostHog).
- **research-intelligence**: market and competitor research, audience segmentation, and keyword/topic intelligence.
- **legal-compliance**: ad-compliance review and regulated-claim gating (overlaps with Legal / Compliance).
- **people-contractors**: creative contractors (designers, editors, video crew) via the People workflow.
- **procurement-supply**: media-buy and vendor management for paid placements and tools.
- **tooling-it**: marketing-stack integration, tag management, and data plumbing.

## Agents and roles (default roster)

- **Marketing Lead**: owns positioning, campaign portfolio, budget allocation, and performance accountability.
- **Positioning Strategist**: translates the offer and audience into a defensible positioning frame; owns the brand brief.
- **Copywriter**: drafts ad copy, email sequences, landing pages, and social posts; iterates against clarity-check and conversion-check.
- **Creative Director**: sets visual direction and brand voice; runs brand-check before launch.
- **Analytics Analyst**: tracks conversion and channel fit; produces the performance memo and kill/scale recommendations.

### Inspectors (quality gates for marketing work)

- **brand-check**: verifies copy and creative against the brand brief and voice standards.
- **clarity-check**: verifies the offer, value prop, and CTA are unambiguous to the target audience.
- **conversion-check**: verifies the funnel, CTA, and landing experience are set up to convert and measure.
- **compliance-check**: verifies claims, disclosures, and platform-policy compliance before launch.
- **channel-fit**: verifies the creative and format match the target channel's conventions and constraints.

## Human approval gates (universal)

Human approval is required for any action that materially changes the company's public promise, brand, or spend.

- **offer-positioning**: any change to how the core offer is positioned to the market.
- **brand-claim**: any public brand promise or high-visibility claim.
- **budget**: any campaign spend above the defined threshold.
- **launch-date**: confirmation to go live on a campaign or launch.
- **final-copy**: sign-off on the final published copy after all inspections pass.

## Tool manifest (minimal viable set)

This department should be able to operate with a small set of tools that provide durable state and evidence.

- **Work surface**: Paperclip (campaigns, approvals, performance memos) or equivalent task tracker

If a company adopts platform-specific tooling, map it here (examples):

- **Email**: beehiiv, Mailchimp, ConvertKit

- **Analytics**: GA4, PostHog, Dub

## Smoke tests / evidence checks

These are fast checks to prove the department is "alive" and aligned.

- **Brand brief exists**: a brand brief or positioning doc exists and is referenced from active campaigns.
- **Campaign brief exists**: at least one recent campaign-brief with objective, audience, offer, budget, and success metric.
- **Inspector verdicts logged**: recent brand-check / clarity-check / conversion-check / compliance-check / channel-fit verdicts archived.
- **Performance memo exists**: at least one performance-review memo with results vs goal and a kill/hold/scale decision.
- **Approval gates are explicit**: offer-positioning, brand-claim, budget, launch-date, and final-copy gates are listed and used.
- **Launch artifacts archived**: published copy, creative, and channel links are stored against each campaign.

## Cross-company mappings (how Marketing manifests by company type)

### Software company

- **Primary focus**: product marketing, landing pages, product-led growth activation, and developer/adopter funnels.
- **Key decisions**: positioning frame, channel mix (PLG vs sales-led), pricing-page copy, activation hooks.
- **KPIs**: activation rate, CAC, trial-to-paid, channel ROAS, landing-page conversion.
- **Extra gates**: compliance-check on security/uptime claims; final-copy on pricing page.

### Physical product company

- **Primary focus**: product pages, retail copy, ad creative, and marketplace listings.
- **Key decisions**: product-page structure, ad creative direction, marketplace copy, promotional calendar.
- **KPIs**: ROAS, listing conversion, ad-attributed sales, return rate from ad traffic.
- **Extra gates**: compliance-check on regulated product claims; brand-claim on packaging copy.

### Local service company

- **Primary focus**: local SEO, reviews, service-area marketing, and lead-to-book conversion.
- **Key decisions**: service-area scope, local-SEO keywords, review-acquisition flow, ad geo-targeting.
- **KPIs**: lead-to-book rate, cost per lead, local ranking, review velocity, on-time arrival perception.
- **Extra gates**: compliance-check on guaranteed-outcome claims; budget on local ad spend.

### Creator company

- **Primary focus**: personal brand, sponsorship pitches, and owned-funnel growth (email/list).
- **Key decisions**: content pillars, sponsorship rate card, funnel design, cross-platform repurposing.
- **KPIs**: reach, watch time/opens, subscriber growth, RPM/CPM, sponsor conversion.
- **Extra gates**: compliance-check on sponsorship disclosures; brand-claim on sponsor alignment.

### Publishing company

- **Primary focus**: book marketing, launch sequencing, and backlist reactivation.
- **Key decisions**: launch calendar, ad spend allocation, series read-through strategy, backlist promos.
- **KPIs**: sell-through, conversion rate, email list growth, read-through, ad ROAS.
- **Extra gates**: brand-claim on cover/metadata positioning; budget on paid ad spend thresholds.

### Education / community company

- **Primary focus**: course marketing, cohort enrollment, and community growth.
- **Key decisions**: enrollment funnel, cohort vs evergreen messaging, scholarship/promo policy, community outreach.
- **KPIs**: enrollment conversion, cost per enrollment, completion-driven retention, referral rate, community growth.
- **Extra gates**: compliance-check on outcome/certification claims; budget on enrollment ad spend.

## Foreman integration notes (recommended)

### Stage model for Marketing tasks

```text
brief → positioning → copy-draft → creative-direction → launch (human gate) → performance-review
```
**Loop mode**: `lean` — standard execution loop with inspection gates at workflow stage boundaries.

### Context packet requirements (marketing runs)

- company-brief (mission, audience, brand voice)
- active-task (the campaign or asset under work)
- role-instructions (which builder + inspector chain runs)
- relevant-artifacts (brand brief, prior campaign briefs, performance memos)
- constraints (budget, channel, launch date, platform rules)
- prior-inspection-results (recent brand-check / clarity-check / conversion-check / compliance-check / channel-fit verdicts)
- human-decisions (outstanding approvals: offer-positioning, brand-claim, budget, launch-date, final-copy)
- expected-output-schema (campaign-brief, launch-artifacts, performance-memo)
