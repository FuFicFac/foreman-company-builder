# Executive / Strategy (Universal Department)

## Purpose

Set company direction and keep the organization aligned: mission, business model, priorities, decision rights, operating cadence, and escalation policy — with durable evidence of decisions and measurable outcomes.

This department is the **policy + prioritization layer** that enables every other department to operate without confusion, thrash, or silent risk.

## Universal responsibilities

- **Mission + positioning**: define/maintain what the company is, who it serves, and the promise it makes.
- **Business model**: how the company creates value, captures value (pricing/revenue), and sustains delivery (cost/constraints).
- **Strategy as choices**: what we will do, what we will not do, and why.
- **Prioritization + sequencing**: translate strategy into an ordered plan with explicit trade-offs.
- **Operating cadence**: weekly/monthly/quarterly rhythms for planning, reviews, and course-correction.
- **Decision rights + escalation**: who decides what, when to ask a human, when to stop, and how to resolve conflicts.
- **Risk management**: maintain a risk register and enforce “stop the line” gates for high-stakes actions.
- **Resource allocation**: time/budget allocation across initiatives; kill-switch for low-leverage work.
- **Cross-department alignment**: ensure product/service, ops, marketing, sales, finance, legal, and quality are working from the same set of truths.

## Core workflows

### 1) Strategy cycle (quarterly / monthly)

**Inputs**
- current scorecard/KPIs
- customer + market evidence (from research)
- capacity/budget constraints
- open risks and escalations

**Process**
- define 1–3 outcomes (north-star + near-term)
- select a small set of initiatives
- set kill-criteria and stop conditions
- publish priorities and decision rights

**Outputs (evidence artifacts)**
- strategy memo (1–2 pages)
- quarterly priorities (top 3–7)
- initiative list with owners, success metrics, and stop conditions

### 2) Weekly operating review

**Inputs**
- KPI/scorecard deltas
- pipeline/launch status
- delivery/ops bottlenecks
- incidents/support escalations

**Process**
- review scorecard
- decide 1–3 course corrections
- remove blockers and reallocate capacity

**Outputs**
- weekly plan (what changed / why)
- updated decision log entries
- escalations + assigned unblock owners

### 3) Decision logging (continuous)

**When to log**
- any decision that changes direction, scope, promises, budget, or risk

**Minimum log record**
- decision + date
- alternatives considered
- rationale
- expected impact + metric
- owner + review date

## Required capabilities (department-level)

- **Strategic clarity**: convert ambiguous goals into explicit outcomes and trade-offs.
- **Prioritization**: sequence work and say “no” with reasons.
- **Operating cadence**: maintain reviews, planning rituals, and follow-ups.
- **Decision hygiene**: decision log, owners, review dates, kill criteria.
- **Escalation + approvals**: know when to stop and ask.
- **Risk framing**: identify, classify, and respond to risks with gates.

## Optional capabilities

- **Portfolio management** (multi-product / multi-brand)
- **Pricing & packaging strategy**
- **Partnership strategy**
- **Org design + hiring plan**
- **Scenario planning**
- **M&A / acquisition scanning** (holding-company contexts)

## Agents and roles (default roster)

- **Executive CEO (Strategy Lead)**: owns mission, priorities, decision rights, resource allocation.
- **Chief of Staff**: runs cadence, captures decisions, enforces follow-up, coordinates cross-department handoffs.
- **Strategy Analyst**: drafts memos, synthesizes evidence, tracks KPI deltas and initiative performance.
- **Risk Steward**: maintains risk register; triggers stop-the-line and approval gates.

### Inspectors (quality gates for executive work)

- **Strategic-fit inspector**: verifies decisions align with mission, constraints, and declared priorities.
- **Prioritization inspector**: flags over-commitment, unclear sequencing, missing trade-offs.
- **Risk inspector**: flags high-stakes actions lacking gates, evidence, or escalation owner.
- **Cadence inspector**: flags missing review rhythms, stale decisions, or unowned initiatives.

## Human approval gates (universal)

Human approval is required for any action that materially changes the company’s promise, risk, or spend.

- **Mission/positioning change**
- **Pricing change** (or discount policy changes)
- **Material spend / budget** (threshold defined per company)
- **Legal/compliance risk** (regulated claims, contracts, privacy/PII handling)
- **Irreversible or destructive actions** (data deletion, account closures, vendor termination)
- **Public brand promises / high-visibility launches**

## Tool manifest (minimal viable set)

This department should be able to operate with a small set of tools that provide durable state and evidence.

- **Work surface**: Paperclip (issues, approvals, decisions) or equivalent task tracker
- **Knowledge base**: repo `docs/` (strategy memo, decision log, risk register)
- **Planning**: calendar + weekly agenda notes
- **Numbers**: spreadsheet tool for scorecards and unit economics
- **Research intake** (optional but common): web search tooling + source archive

If a company adopts platform-specific tooling, map it here (examples):

- **Project delivery**: Linear, Jira
- **Docs**: Notion, Google Docs
- **Analytics**: GA4, PostHog, Stripe, Shopify dashboards
- **Comms**: Slack, email

## Smoke tests / evidence checks

These are fast checks to prove the department is “alive” and aligned.

- **Mission exists**: a single paragraph mission + target customer + promise.
- **Operating cadence exists**: a weekly review note and a quarterly strategy memo template.
- **Decision log exists**: at least 3 recent decisions with owners and review dates.
- **Priority list exists**: top priorities with success metrics and stop conditions.
- **Risk register exists**: top risks with severity, mitigations, and escalation owners.
- **Approval gates are explicit**: “what requires a human” is listed and used.

## Cross-company mappings (how Executive / Strategy manifests by company type)

### Software company

- **Primary focus**: product strategy, roadmap, reliability posture, go-to-market sequencing.
- **Key decisions**: roadmap trade-offs, technical direction, pricing, launch gates, incident response posture.
- **KPIs**: activation, retention, revenue, churn, uptime, defect rates, cycle time.
- **Extra gates**: production deploy policy; security risk and data handling approvals.

### Physical product company

- **Primary focus**: unit economics, supply chain risk, inventory strategy, quality and returns.
- **Key decisions**: vendor selection thresholds, MOQ/lead times, warranty/returns policy, pricing and margin floors.
- **KPIs**: gross margin, return rate, on-time fulfillment, defect rate, cash conversion cycle.
- **Extra gates**: purchase orders beyond threshold; regulatory and safety claims.

### Local service company

- **Primary focus**: capacity planning, service quality, scheduling, geographic scope, reviews/referrals.
- **Key decisions**: service menu, pricing, hiring/contractor strategy, routing/scheduling rules.
- **KPIs**: utilization, lead-to-book rate, on-time arrival, NPS/reviews, repeat rate.
- **Extra gates**: refunds/complaints policy changes; safety/liability escalation.

### Creator company

- **Primary focus**: audience strategy, creative direction, consistency, platform risk.
- **Key decisions**: content pillars, posting cadence, sponsorship policy, brand boundaries.
- **KPIs**: reach, watch time/opens, subscriber growth, RPM/CPM, sponsor conversion.
- **Extra gates**: sponsor claims and brand alignment approvals; platform policy compliance.

### Publishing company

- **Primary focus**: editorial pipeline, positioning, launch sequencing, direct-sales vs platform mix.
- **Key decisions**: genre positioning, pricing, cover direction approvals, launch calendar, inventory/format mix.
- **KPIs**: sell-through, conversion rate, refund rate, email list growth, read-through (series).
- **Extra gates**: cover direction and metadata positioning approvals; paid ad spend thresholds.

### Education / community company

- **Primary focus**: learning outcomes, retention, community health, curriculum roadmap.
- **Key decisions**: curriculum scope, cohort vs evergreen format, pricing tiers, moderation policy.
- **KPIs**: completion rate, retention, referrals, community engagement, support burden.
- **Extra gates**: policy changes (moderation/refunds), accreditation or regulated claims.

## Foreman integration notes (recommended)

### Stage model for Executive / Strategy tasks

```text
intake → clarify → propose → decide (human gate as needed) → communicate → follow-up → review
```

### Context packet requirements (executive runs)

- mission + positioning snapshot
- current priorities + initiative list
- latest scorecard/KPIs
- active decision needing resolution
- constraints (time/budget/people/tools)
- risk register (top risks)
- prior decisions that constrain the choice
- expected output schema (memo, decision record, updated priorities)
