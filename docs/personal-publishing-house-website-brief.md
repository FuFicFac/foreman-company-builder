# Personal Publishing House Website Brief

This is the handoff brief for a web/design agent. It intentionally hides most of the infrastructure until after the audience, promise, and product shape are clear.

## Designer prompt

Design a warm, author-friendly landing section/site for **Personal Publishing House**, the flagship **Foreman Company** for writers.

Foreman is the parent product. A Foreman Company is the branded package/category. Personal Publishing House is the first launch example.

Audience: introverted writers, authors, solo creators, and newsletter/book people who want to write but need help with editing coordination, metadata, launch planning, reader follow-up, and operational tasks.

Primary promise:

> **Start where you are. Get a publishing workflow around it.**

The page must not imply the author has to start by dropping in a draft. The user can enter at any stage: idea, outline, partial draft, finished manuscript, editing, metadata, launch, reader follow-up, analytics, or relaunch.

Tone: gentle, literary, practical, empowering. Not technical-first. Not corporate SaaS. Not cyberpunk.

Visual style: soft pastels + earth tones, warm ivory/paper backgrounds, sage, dusty rose, terracotta, lavender, powder blue, clay, warm charcoal. Use manuscript cards, editorial notes, publishing pipeline visuals, calm progress ribbons, and subtle paper grain.

## Homepage hierarchy

### 1. Hero

Headline:

> **Start where you are.**

Subhead:

> Personal Publishing House is a Foreman Company for writers. Whether you have an idea, a rough draft, a finished manuscript, or a launch already in motion, it finds your stage and builds the next publishing workflow around it.

Primary CTA:

> Start with Hermes

Secondary CTA:

> See the manuscript workflow

### 2. Writer problem

Make the user feel seen:

> You already did the hardest part: you made something. Now it needs editing, metadata, launch planning, reader follow-up, and a dozen tiny operational steps that can pull you away from the work itself.

### 3. Product promise

> Personal Publishing House gives your manuscript a small, visible publishing team. It organizes the pipeline, checks its tools, surfaces blockers, and tells you what needs your attention.

### 4. Manuscript workflow visual

Show this as a beautiful pipeline, ribbon, desk map, or manuscript path:

```text
Draft → Intake → Edit → Proof → Metadata → Launch → Reader follow-up
```

### 5. Paperclip preview

Explain Paperclip only after the user understands the workflow.

> Paperclip is where you see inside the company: which agents are working, which tasks are blocked, what stage your manuscript is in, and what needs your decision.

### 6. Daily check-in

Headline:

> Every morning, your company checks in.

Copy:

> The daily check-in tells you whether the company is ready, what is blocked, which tools are missing, and what needs your attention.

Include a status card:

- Green: ready
- Yellow: usable, needs attention
- Red: blocked

### 7. Install choice

Headline:

> Choose your runtime

Primary card:

**Install with Hermes**

> Recommended. Hermes runs the agents, tools, skills, scheduled checks, and follow-up that make Personal Publishing House feel alive.

Secondary card:

**Install with OpenClaw**

> OpenClaw is supported as an alternate path, but it may require more assembly. Choose this if you already know OpenClaw and are comfortable adding missing tools or connectors yourself.

### 8. How it works, quietly

Only after the user-facing product is clear, explain:

- Paperclip shows the company.
- Hermes runs the company.
- Foreman provides the publishing discipline and templates.
- Printing Press gives agents tools.
- Each book gets a local workspace and living wiki.
- OpenClaw is an advanced alternate runtime.

Do not lead with this section.

## Required sections

- Hero
- Writer problem
- Manuscript workflow
- Paperclip interface preview
- “What needs you today?” card
- Daily check-in / heartbeat section
- Install with Hermes / Install with OpenClaw
- FAQ for nontechnical authors

## FAQ topics

- Do I need to be technical?
- Will this write my book for me?
- What is Hermes?
- What is Paperclip?
- What is an Personal Publishing House?
- What happens to my manuscript?
- Can I use my existing notes and research?
- Do I need a Second Brain?
- Can I use OpenClaw?
- Can I run this locally?

Important FAQ answer:

> You do not need a Second Brain. Each book gets a simple local workspace and living wiki that the agents manage in the background.

## Non-goals

- Do not make the stack the brand.
- Do not lead with infrastructure diagrams.
- Do not require Second Brain.
- Do not mention Super Hank Chat.
- Do not pitch a database.
- Do not make authors feel like they must become engineers.

## Success criteria

A writer should leave the page understanding:

1. This is for manuscripts and publishing workflows.
2. It helps them stay focused on writing.
3. Paperclip is where they see what is happening.
4. Hermes is the recommended install path.
5. OpenClaw is possible but more DIY.
6. Each book gets a simple workspace and living wiki.
7. The company checks in daily with status and blockers.
